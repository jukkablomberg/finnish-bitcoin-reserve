I am writing because [previously known policy interest, e.g. "of your work on the Finance Committee" / "your published views on monetary policy diversification" / "your role at Suomen Pankki on reserves management"].

Today, 13 May 2026, I am publishing an independent policy whitepaper titled *Finland's Path to a Sovereign Bitcoin Reserve: Operating Model, Custody, Political Sequencing*. It is, to my knowledge, the first Finnish-authored public framework on this question.

**Why this matters now:**
- Sweden's opposition filed a parliamentary motion in October 2024 for a national Bitcoin reserve. The motion's reasoning section explicitly names Finland and Norway as the comparator states Sweden risks falling behind — placing Finland's leadership on this question on the Swedish parliamentary record.
- The United States established a Strategic Bitcoin Reserve via Executive Order in March 2025, with a Treasury purchase mechanism signalled as imminent.
- The Czech National Bank Governor publicly considered Bitcoin as a reserve diversification component in January 2025 — the first sitting EU central-bank governor on the record.
- Switzerland's citizen-initiative path failed in May 2026, confirming that this is a top-down policy question.
- The European Central Bank is silent because the composition of member-state reserves is a national competence under EU treaties, not a monetary policy matter.
- Finland has not yet defined its position on a question Sweden has named Finland into.

**What the whitepaper proposes:**
A three-track approach — (A) establish a published governance framework for state-held digital assets, which Finland currently lacks; (B) establish the legal mechanism for permanent sovereign accumulation through a one-time amendment to the Confiscation Act; (C) optionally allocate 0.5% of FX reserves to Bitcoin over five years. Plus a three-tier custody architecture and political sequencing across Finnish parties.

## Etiquette notes

1. **Do not BCC.** Each email goes individually. This is a serious policy document; bulk-blasting is the wrong register.
2. **Do not attach the PDF.** Link to the landing page. The recipient can choose to download.
3. **Do not follow up aggressively.** Send once. If no reply in 14 days, you can send a single one-line follow-up: "Just wanted to check this didn't get caught in a filter — happy to discuss if useful." After that, leave it alone.
4. **If you get a reply, respond same-day if possible.** Acknowledge, offer the meeting, no oversell.
5. **Track responses in a simple spreadsheet.** Name, role, date sent, date replied, outcome. This is also the seed of your HHJ-trajectory contact list.

**Why this matters now:**
- Sweden's opposition filed a formal parliamentary motion in October 2024 for a national Bitcoin reserve, **explicitly naming Finland and Norway in the reasoning section as the comparator states Sweden risks falling behind**. That naming is the political detail — Sweden has nominated Finland's leadership in writing, on a parliamentary record.
- The U.S. established a Strategic Bitcoin Reserve via Executive Order in March 2025 and is signalling a Treasury purchase mechanism is imminent.
- The Czech National Bank Governor publicly considered Bitcoin as a reserve diversification component in January 2025 — the first sitting EU central-bank governor on the record.
- Switzerland's grassroots referendum failed in May 2026 — confirming that this is a top-down policy question, not a bottom-up citizen initiative.
- The European Central Bank is silent because the composition of member-state reserves is a national competence under the Treaty on the Functioning of the European Union, not an ECB question.
- Finland has not yet defined its position on a question Sweden has named Finland into.

**What the whitepaper proposes:**
Three sequential tracks — (A) establish a published governance framework for state-held digital assets, which Finland currently lacks; (B) establish the legal mechanism for permanent sovereign accumulation through a one-time amendment to the Confiscation Act; (C) optionally allocate 0.5% of FX reserves to Bitcoin over five years. Plus a three-tier custody architecture, political sequencing tailored to each major Finnish party, and replies to the five most common counter-arguments.

**Why I wrote it:**
I was CMO at two international cryptocurrency exchanges between 2017 and 2024, working with regulators across Europe, Asia, and the Middle East. I've seen the question come up in capital after capital, with the same gap each time: no domestic operator has written down what their country should actually do. This is that document, for Finland.
